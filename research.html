Variations and relations

One could say that the word relative, of utmost importance in contemporary mathematics, was firmly placed in the horizon of mathematical practice by Cohen and Grothendieck. The first one continued the hit given by Godel's incompleteness theorems, and introduced us to the world of relative consistency proofs. A world that somehow Godel denied. Godels stance was a extremelly particular one. One that could be taged by the term "intuitionist idealist". On the other hand, Grothendieck taught us many lessons, one of them begin that to study an object one should study its variations: what one could call the relative situation. (And evident heritage of Riemann.) I am deeply influenced by the relative stance, be it in the flavour of moduli problems, or in the flavor of topoi. And my incipient but heartfelt research tries to show that, and will explore it further in the future.

Cryptography and networks

I am currently working on blockchain in its theoretical and practical aspects. I am interested in the mathematical aspects of cryptography, and in the applications of cryptography to networks. In the theoretical aspect, I am interested in studying isogenies of elliptic curves and abelian varieties, and their applications to cryptography, and also discrete logarithm problems in the context of isogenies. I am also interested in the applications of cryptography to the social networks in the colombian context to promote a better control of resources and information against corruption.
